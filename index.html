Teaching notes

  1. Tax shields lower the effective cost of debt.
  2. Higher leverage raises equity risk, so equity return rises faster than debt falls.
  3. Distress costs create an interior optimum instead of “more debt is always better,” which would make class discussion unreasonably short.
Model structure: WACC = E/V × Re + D/V × Rd × (1 - tax rate) + distress cost.

Decision rule

The preferred leverage is the debt ratio that minimizes WACC in this simplified framework. In practice, students should also consider covenants, refinancing risk, agency costs, and market comparables.
